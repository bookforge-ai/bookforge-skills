# Takedown, Opt-Out, and DMCA Policy

> **Are you an author or rights holder who wants content removed?** Scroll to [the quick path](#quick-path-for-authors-and-rights-holders) below. You do not need a lawyer and you do not need to file a formal DMCA notice unless you want to. One email is enough.

BookForge is a non-commercial, open-source project that distills non-fiction books into agent skills. We take legitimate takedown requests seriously, respond quickly, and honor opt-outs without argument. This document explains how to request removal and what to expect from us.

---

## Quick path for authors and rights holders

If you are the author, publisher, or designated rights holder for a book that appears in this repository, and you want your book's skills removed:

1. Send one email to the address in the [Contact](#contact) section below.
2. Include: the book title, your relationship to the work (author, publisher, agent), and a statement that you want the skills removed.
3. We will acknowledge within 48 hours and remove the content within 48 hours of verification.

You do not need to cite a statute. You do not need a lawyer. You do not need to prove infringement. An opt-out request from a legitimate rights holder is honored on request. This is a commitment, not a negotiation.

If you would prefer a formal DMCA notice, the full procedure is described in the [DMCA notice](#dmca-notice-17-usc-ยง512c3) section.

---

## Our response commitments

When we receive a takedown request or DMCA notice, we commit to the following:

- **Acknowledge within 48 hours** of receipt (business days and weekends included).
- **Act within 48 hours** of verification. Verification usually means confirming the requester is the rights holder or their authorized representative.
- **Remove in good faith.** We do not second-guess opt-out requests from verified rights holders. If you want your book removed, we remove it.
- **Keep a compliance log.** Every takedown and opt-out is recorded privately so our response history is auditable.
- **No retaliation.** We will not file penalty counter-claims against authors who exercise their right to opt out.

If we fail to meet these commitments, please email us directly and we will escalate internally.

## DMCA notice (17 USC §512(c)(3))

A formal DMCA notice must include all of the following elements. If any are missing, we may ask you to resubmit with the missing information — we will not ignore a notice, but we cannot act on an incomplete one.

1. **A physical or electronic signature** of the rights holder or their authorized representative.
2. **Identification of the copyrighted work** claimed to be infringed. For BookForge, this usually means naming the book by title, author, and ISBN.
3. **Identification of the material claimed to be infringing.** Please link directly to the specific SKILL.md file or directory within this repository. Vague claims like "your project infringes my book" require us to spend time identifying which files you mean, which delays the response.
4. **Your contact information** — address, telephone number, and email address.
5. **A statement under penalty of perjury** that you have a good-faith belief that the use is not authorized by the rights holder, their agent, or the law.
6. **A statement under penalty of perjury** that the information in the notice is accurate and that you are authorized to act on behalf of the rights holder.

Send the complete notice to the email address in the [designated agent](#designated-agent-for-dmca-notices) section above.

## Counter-notice procedure (for contributors)

If your contribution was removed from this repository in response to a DMCA notice and you believe the removal was a mistake or a misidentification, you can file a counter-notice. A counter-notice must include:

1. **Your physical or electronic signature.**
2. **Identification of the material** that was removed and its former location in the repository.
3. **A statement under penalty of perjury** that you have a good-faith belief that the material was removed by mistake or misidentification.
4. **Your contact information** — address, telephone number, and email address.
5. **A statement consenting to the jurisdiction** of the federal district court for the judicial district in which your address is located (or, if outside the United States, any judicial district in which BookForge may be found) and that you will accept service of process from the original notifier.

Send counter-notices to the email address in the [designated agent](#designated-agent-for-dmca-notices) section above.

Upon receiving a valid counter-notice, we will forward it to the original notifier. If the notifier does not file a court action within 10 business days, we will restore the removed material.

## Non-DMCA opt-out (for authors and rights holders who prefer a faster path)

Formal DMCA notices exist for situations where the rights holder wants a paper trail and legal protection. Most authors do not need this. If you are an author who would rather just have your book removed without filing formal paperwork, send us an email and we will honor the request.

Send the email to the address in the [designated agent](#designated-agent-for-dmca-notices) section above. Include:

- The book title.
- Your relationship to the work (author, publisher, literary agent, estate representative).
- A simple statement that you want the skills for this book removed.

We will verify that you are who you say you are (usually through a second email or a publicly-listed author contact) and remove the content within 48 hours. No lawyers, no statutes, no argument.

You can also request that the content *not be re-added* if a future contributor attempts to submit new skills for the same book. We maintain an opt-out registry for this purpose.

## Author-verified badge program (for authors who want to participate)

If you are the author of a book in this library and you would prefer to *participate* rather than opt out, we offer an author-verified badge program. Authors who review their book's skills and confirm they accurately represent the book's frameworks receive a verified badge on their skill set and are credited prominently.

This is entirely optional and separate from takedown. Contact us at the same email address if you are interested.

## Frequently asked questions

**What happens between the time I send a takedown request and the time the content is removed?**
We commit to removing within 48 hours of verification. During that window, the content remains in the repository because we cannot remove it faster than we can confirm your identity. If you need faster action for a specific reason, say so in your email and we will prioritize it.

**Do I need a lawyer?**
No. We honor takedown and opt-out requests from verified rights holders without requiring legal formality. We offer the formal DMCA path for rights holders who want one.

**What if I am not sure whether I am the rights holder?**
Contact us anyway and explain your situation. We will work through it with you. We would rather discuss an unclear situation than accidentally ignore a legitimate concern.

**Will you counter-sue or retaliate against me for filing a takedown or opt-out?**
No. Exercising your rights as an author or rights holder is not something we retaliate against, and we commit to this publicly.

**What if I want my book removed now but added back later under the author-verified badge program?**
Yes, we can do this. Send the removal request first; once your book is removed, we can discuss the verified badge program on a separate track.

**What if my takedown notice is incomplete or the information is wrong?**
We will reply to let you know what is missing or unclear. We will not ignore the notice.
